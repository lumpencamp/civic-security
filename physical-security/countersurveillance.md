# Countersurveillance Basics: Defeating Real-Time Tracking

*Status: Physical Security Tactical Guide | Audience: Field Operatives and Direct Action Teams*

In modern civic engagements, you are not just being watched by human eyes; you are being tracked by an automated, integrated surveillance grid. Countersurveillance is no longer just about losing a tail—it is about blinding the grid.

This guide details the physical and digital convergence necessary to mitigate real-time tracking infrastructure in public spaces.

---

## 1. Immediate Device Lockdown (Biometrics Elimination)

If you are facing imminent detention or entering a high-friction zone, your device must be locked out of biometric access immediately. Police can legally force your fingerprint or face to unlock a device; they cannot force a passphrase without a warrant.

### The Hardware Kill Switch
Do not fumble through settings menus. Use the hardware kill switch to instantly disable biometrics and force the device to require a passcode.

*   **iOS (iPhone):** Press and hold the **Side Button + Either Volume Button** simultaneously for 3 seconds until the "Slide to Power Off" screen appears. Tap 'Cancel'. Biometrics are now disabled.
*   **Android (Pixel/Samsung):** Press and hold the **Power Button + Volume Up** (or just the Power button on older models) to bring up the power menu. Tap **Lockdown**. (You must enable this option beforehand in *Settings > Display > Lock screen > Show lockdown option*).

## 2. The Illusion of Airplane Mode

The most dangerous misconception in operational security is that "Airplane Mode" prevents tracking. It does not.

*   **Cell-Tower Triangulation:** Even in Airplane Mode, your phone's baseband processor may still passively listen to cell towers, and in some jurisdictions, emergency 911 location services remain active, allowing the carrier to triangulate your position.
*   **The Wi-Fi/Bluetooth Sniffer Threat:** Modern smartphones use background Wi-Fi and Bluetooth scanning to improve location accuracy, even when those features appear turned "off" in the quick settings drop-down.
*   **The Mitigation:** Airplane mode is insufficient for true operational anonymity. If you must carry a device, you must manually navigate to Location settings and disable **Wi-Fi Scanning** and **Bluetooth Scanning**. For absolute certainty, the device must be powered down and placed in a **Faraday bag**.

## 3. Auditing the Surveillance Grid

Before and during an operation, you must actively audit your surroundings to identify automated tracking arrays.

### Detecting Automated License Plate Readers (ALPRs)
ALPRs ingest thousands of plates per minute, feeding your location into regional fusion centers.
*   **Identification:** Look for small, boxy cameras with a prominent infrared flash ring, often mounted in pairs angled downward. They are mounted on traffic light poles, toll booths, and increasingly on the trunks/roofs of unmarked municipal vehicles or private repo trucks.
*   **Mitigation:** Do not drive personal or organizational vehicles to operational zones. Park significantly out of the zone and transit the final mile via foot, bicycle, or cash-paid public transit.

### Detecting Facial Recognition Arrays
Facial recognition is deployed via fixed POD cameras and mobile units.
*   **Identification:** High-mounted PTZ (Pan-Tilt-Zoom) dome cameras, or mobile trailers with telescoping camera masts deployed near protest zones.
*   **Mitigation:** Symmetrical disruption. AI facial mapping relies on the symmetry of your eyes, nose, and jawline. A high-quality **N95 mask** paired with **polarized sunglasses** (which block infrared eye tracking) and a low-profile hat is the most effective defense. "Dazzle" makeup is largely obsolete against modern deep-learning models.

## 4. Physical Concealment: Breaking the Tracking Loop

If you suspect you have been flagged by a mobile surveillance unit or an automated tracking loop, you must break the visual continuity.

### The Uniformity Tactic (Black Bloc)
The primary method to break a tracking loop is uniformity. If 50 individuals wear identical, unbranded black clothing (hoodies, pants, masks, sunglasses), it becomes mathematically impossible for an automated system or a human operator to maintain a continuous track on a single target when the crowd moves or shifts.

### The Layered Shed
If you must transit out of a hot zone:
1.  Enter a high-traffic area with multiple exits or a space without camera coverage (e.g., a public restroom stall).
2.  Shed your outer operational layer (e.g., the black hoodie and mask) to reveal distinctly different, brightly colored "civilian" clothing underneath.
3.  Exit the space from a different door, fundamentally altering your visual profile and breaking the continuous track established by the surveillance array.
