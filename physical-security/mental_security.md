# Protest Mental Security & Digital Trauma

Activism is a marathon, not a sprint. While physical and digital security protect your body and your data, mental security protects your capacity to continue the work. The constant exposure to state violence, online harassment, and high-stress organizing can lead to severe burnout and trauma.

## 1. Digital Trauma and Secondary Trauma

You do not have to be physically present at an event to experience trauma. Endlessly scrolling through videos of police brutality or violence against your community (doomscrolling) causes significant psychological harm, known as secondary or vicarious trauma.

*   **Curate Your Feed:** Do not follow accounts that constantly post un-warned graphic violence unless it is strictly necessary for your role (e.g., you are an OSINT researcher).
*   **The 24-Hour Rule:** After a major traumatic event, step away from social media for 24 hours. The initial information is often confusing, inflammatory, and psychologically damaging. Wait for verified reporting.
*   **Turn Off Autoplay:** In Twitter, Instagram, and Reddit, turn off video autoplay settings. You should choose when to engage with graphic content, not have it forced upon you.

## 2. Managing Online Harassment (Doxing)

If your identity becomes known to hostile actors, you may experience a coordinated harassment campaign.

*   **Do Not Panic, Do Not Delete:** In the immediate aftermath of doxing, do not panic-delete your accounts. This can sometimes fuel the harassers. Instead, use the lockdown tools (set to private, disable comments).
*   **The Buddy System:** If your accounts are being flooded with hate speech or threats, hand over the passwords to a trusted friend. Have them log in, document the severe threats (take screenshots for potential legal action), and mass-block the attackers. You should not be the one reading the abuse.
*   **Assess the Physical Threat:** Distinguish between online noise (trolls from another country) and credible physical threats (someone posting your home address and encouraging local action). If the threat is credible, rely on your physical security plan (staying with friends, securing your home).

## 3. Disconnecting as a Security Practice

The expectation of 24/7 availability is a major vulnerability. Organizers who are exhausted make poor security decisions.

*   **Establish "Dark Hours":** Agree on specific times (e.g., 10 PM to 8 AM) where non-emergency group chats are silenced.
*   **Rotate Roles:** High-stress roles (like jail support or media point) should be rotated regularly. No one person should hold these responsibilities indefinitely.
*   **Celebrate Rest:** Treat rest as a tactical necessity. A burnt-out activist is an ineffective activist and a liability to the group's OPSEC.
