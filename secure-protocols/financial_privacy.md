# Financial Privacy for Activists

Activist organizations and high-profile organizers are frequently targeted financially. This can include frozen bank accounts, PayPal bans, Patreon shutdowns, or having transaction histories subpoenaed by law enforcement to map out a group's network.

Financial privacy is critical for organizational resilience.

## 1. Cash is King

For physical security and untraceable transactions, cash remains the best option.

*   **Burner Phones & SIMs:** Always purchase burner phones, SIM cards, and top-up cards in cash. Using a credit card links the phone directly to your identity.
*   **Protest Supplies:** Buy sensitive materials (masks, medical supplies, paint) with cash.
*   **Travel:** If traveling to a sensitive location, use cash to buy transit passes or gas, rather than using an I-PASS or a credit card, which creates a location-tracking paper trail.

## 2. The Danger of Standard Payment Apps

Venmo, PayPal, CashApp, and Zelle are highly insecure for activist use.

*   **Public Feeds:** Venmo's default setting makes transactions public. Ensure all settings are strictly private.
*   **Easy Subpoenas:** These companies readily hand over transaction histories to law enforcement. If you pay someone for a "protest banner" via Venmo, the police now have a documented link between you two.
*   **Arbitrary Bans:** These platforms frequently freeze accounts associated with controversial political organizing, cutting off necessary funds without recourse.

## 3. Cryptocurrencies: Bitcoin vs. Monero

Cryptocurrency is often proposed as a solution, but there is a massive misunderstanding about how it works.

*   **Bitcoin (BTC) is NOT Anonymous:** Bitcoin operates on a public ledger (the blockchain). Every transaction ever made is permanently visible to the public. If an adversary links your real identity to your Bitcoin address (e.g., when you buy it on an exchange like Coinbase), they can see your entire financial history. **Do not use Bitcoin for sensitive transactions.**
*   **Monero (XMR) is the Standard:** Monero is a "privacy coin." Its blockchain uses advanced cryptography to hide the sender, the receiver, and the amount transacted. It is currently the only reliable digital currency for anonymous donations and transactions.
    *   *Usage:* Activist groups should consider accepting donations in Monero to protect the identity of their donors and prevent their funds from being tracked.

## 4. Prepaid Gift Cards

For online purchases where cash isn't an option (like paying for secure web hosting or VPNs), prepaid Visa/Mastercard gift cards bought with cash at a local store are a viable alternative.

*   **Warning:** Many online services are cracking down on prepaid cards and may reject them.
