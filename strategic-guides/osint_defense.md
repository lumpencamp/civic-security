# OSINT Defense: Reducing Your Digital Footprint

Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT) refers to the collection and analysis of information that is publicly available on the internet. Police, corporations, and far-right groups heavily use OSINT to identify, track, and dox activists.

Defending against OSINT means shrinking your digital footprint so that searching your name yields as little useful information as possible.

## 1. Social Media Lockdown

Social media is the primary source of OSINT.

*   **Private Accounts:** Set all personal accounts (Instagram, Twitter, Facebook, TikTok) to private.
*   **Pseudonyms:** Use an alias or a variation of your name for public-facing activist work. Do not link your activist persona to your personal/professional identity.
*   **Prune Friends/Followers:** Regularly review your follower lists. Remove anyone you do not personally know and trust. Opponents often create fake accounts to follow activists and gather intelligence.
*   **Disable Location Tagging:** Never geotag your posts. Turn off location services for camera and social media apps.

## 2. Opting Out of Data Brokers

Data brokers are companies (like Whitepages, Spokeo, LexisNexis, Radaris) that scrape public records (voter registrations, property records, court documents) and sell comprehensive profiles on individuals. These profiles usually include your current and past addresses, phone numbers, email addresses, and the names of your relatives.

*   **Manual Opt-Out:** You have the legal right to request these companies remove your data. You can manually visit each site, find their "Opt-Out" page, and submit a removal request.
    *   *Resource:* Search for "Michael Bazzell Data Broker Workbook" for a comprehensive, free guide on how to manually opt-out of hundreds of data brokers.
*   **Paid Services:** If you have the budget, services like **DeleteMe**, **Kanary**, or **Incogni** will automate this process and continuously monitor brokers to remove your information.

## 3. Scrubbing Search Results

*   **Google Alerts:** Set up a Google Alert for your real name, your common usernames, and your phone number. This will notify you if your information is published somewhere new.
*   **The "Right to be Forgotten":** If sensitive personal information (like your home address) is posted maliciously on a website, you can submit a request to Google to have that specific page removed from their search results (search for "Google remove personally identifiable information").

## 4. Compartmentalization of Identities

Create strict firewalls between different areas of your life.

*   **Use Dedicated Emails:** Have one email address for banking/bills, a separate one for personal friends, and a completely different, pseudonymous email (e.g., ProtonMail) for activist work.
*   **Virtual Phone Numbers:** Do not give out your real cell phone number for activism. Use VoIP services like Google Voice (requires a Google account) or more private alternatives like MySudo or a dedicated burner SIM for organizing.
